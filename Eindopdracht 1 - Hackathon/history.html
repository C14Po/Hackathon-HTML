Soundtracks: a history

Incidental music, classical and the silent era

Incidental music was written to accompany stage plays. Many of Shakespeare’s works incorporated incidental music. Bij the 19th century such music was commonplace. When silent films began to appear (mid 1890's-1920's), music was the most important way to set the mood, with film scores being improvised in real time. The first score specifically written for a film was by famous composer Camille Saint-Saëns. The planets (1916) an orchestral suite bij Gustav Holst, has been of great influence on more recent composers, notably John Williams (Star Wars Imperial March) and Hans Zimmmer (Gladiator). To listen: Gustav Holst - Jupiter (from the Planets)

The Golden Age and Experimentalism

“Talkies” soon appeared, and music formed an integral part of the soundtrack. The first completely original score for a movie with sound was composed by Max Steiner, for “King Kong” in 1933. The 40's and 50's is a unique period in the history of film scoring, where original songs were written for the movies. Composers during this period primarily come from backgrounds in concert music, and almost exclusively write orchestral pieces, though jazz sounds start to make an entrance. In the 1960's rock music appeared in films. Composers would experiment with instruments and new sounds. To listen: Henry Mancini - Pink Panther (Jazz sound)

Synthesized sound and minimalist approach

In the 1970s New Hollywood era, film music heads in two concurrent, divergent directions with the incorporation of synthesizers and a return to classic scoring. John Williams single-handedly revives the sound of golden age Hollywood (orchestral) with his score for Star Wars. Synthesized sound in films first occurred in the 1980s and it revolutionized film scoring. This meant that an entire film could be scored using just one performer. Today there are composers who tend towards a more minimalistic approach to composing, such as Hans Zimmer. He continues to be influential, thanks to his extensive and innovative use of technology and hyper-collaborative approach to music production. To listen: Vangelis - Blade Runner theme (synth)
